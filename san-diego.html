    • Daniel Jackson

      Daniel Jackson was a saxophonist, pianist, composer, and music teacher. Inspired to play sax at a young age (by Harold Land who played in a band with his elder brother, Fred, and who later played with the Clifford Brown Quartet), Jackson's mother paid for his lessons on a maid's wage. When she could no longer afford those, Jackson made a deal with his teacher, agreeing to mow his lawn in exchange for lessons.

      After graduating, Jackson joined the navy, where he met and played with among others Wes Montgomery.

      "I had a lot of fun playing with Wes [Montgomery] when I was in the Air Force. I think he was a postman somewhere. I was just sittin‚Äô in. I would write music for him because he couldn‚Äôt read or write [music]. He‚Äôd come up with some tune and he‚Äôd say, ‚ÄòHey, man, can you write some notes down for me?‚Äô And I‚Äôd do that.‚Äù

      After the war, he toured Europe with the Ray Charles Band.

      ‚ÄúHe (Charles) gave us a concert once,‚Äù says Jackson. ‚ÄúIt was terrifying. The saxophone was so hot, steam heat was rising from it, and melting into these golden globes of tone.‚Äù

      Jackson returned to SD and went on to play with and teach those that would later become the greats: bassists Bob Magnusson and Mark Dresser, trumpeter Gilbert Castellanos, pianist Mike Wofford, sax symbol Charles McPherson, flutist Holly Hofmann, and pianist-singer A.J. Croce.

      In January 2010, San Diego City Councilwoman Donna Frye officially declared the week of January 24 to be Daniel Jackson Week. At the time Jackson was on Sabbatical, and Frye, a long time fan, wanted to make sure that his music was always celebrated.

      Daniel Jackson passed away in 2014.

      Peter Sprague

      Peter Sprague was born in 1955 in Cleveland, Ohio, moved to Colorado and in 1963 the family moved to Del Mar, CA. He went to the Interlochen Arts Academy, studied with Pat Matheny in Boston, and went on tour with great musicians like Chick Corea, Hubert Laws, Dianne Reeves, Sergio Mendes and others. From 1990 to 1993 Peter taught music at both California Institute of the Arts in Los Angeles and the Musicians Institute in Hollywood. He continues to teach every summer at the annual UCSD Jazz Camp in La Jolla, CA. In 1994, Sprague opened SpragueLand recording studio in Encinitas. He describes it as the "West coast cousin" to Jimi Hendrix's Electric LadyLand in NYC.
